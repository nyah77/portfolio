Typography Explained

Typography is the art and technique of arranging type to make written language legible, readable, and visually appealing. It includes the selection of typefaces, point size, line length, line spacing, and letter spacing, as well as the adjustment of space between pairs of letters.

Key Typography Terms

Bracket
The curved or angled connection between a serif and the main stroke of a letter.
Terminal
The end of a stroke that does not have a serif.
Serif
Small projections or "feet" at the ends of strokes in certain typefaces.
Baseline
The invisible line on which most characters sit.
Ascender Line
The guideline that marks the uppermost point of an ascender, which is the part of a lowercase letter extending above the x-height.
Descender Line
The guideline indicating the lowest point of a descender, which is the portion of a lowercase letter that extends below the baseline.
X-height
The height of lowercase letters, typically measured by the lowercase "x."
Loop
A fully closed or partially closed counter at the bottom of certain lowercase letters, such as "g."
Stem
The primary vertical or diagonal stroke of a letterform, providing structural support.
Arm
A horizontal or upward-slanting stroke that does not connect to another stroke at one end, as seen in letters like "T" and "Y."
