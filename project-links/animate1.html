Animate project 1

Project Summary

The purpose of this project was to practice creating an original animation in Adobe Animate while learning how to use the program’s tools and techniques effectively. By designing a character and placing her in a scene with a background, I was able to apply key skills such as working with shapes, converting artwork into symbols, and animating movement through frame-by-frame techniques. This project also allowed me to experiment with different animation methods, such as motion tweens and parenting, before deciding on the approach that worked best. The goal was not only to complete a short animation but also to build a deeper understanding of the software.

Explanation of tools and skills

For my Adobe Animate project, I created a short animation featuring a girl character on a background. To build the character, I used basic vector drawing tools in Animate, such as the Oval, Rectangle, and Brush tools, combining and reshaping them to form her body, head, arms, and other details. Working with shapes and fills allowed me to layer and adjust her parts so they looked like one cohesive figure. Once the main artwork was finished, I converted parts of the girl into symbols (like arms or the body) so that I could move and animate them more easily without redrawing. Using symbols also helped keep the project organized in the Library. For the animation process, I first experimented with the parenting technique and motion tweens to animate the girl’s arm. However, I ran into some trouble making the movements look natural, so I decided to switch to frame-by-frame animation instead. This gave me more direct control over each pose and made the motion feel smoother. The background came from Animate’s built-in assets, which I imported onto the stage and arranged behind the girl.
